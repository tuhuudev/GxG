# The Secretary Bird: A Snake Hunter That Kills With Its Feet

Most birds of prey kill with talons and a dive from the sky. The secretary bird does something stranger and far more precise: it hunts venomous snakes on the ground and kills them by stomping. And the speed of that strike is what turns a long-legged bird into one of nature's deadliest snake hunters.

## A Kick Faster Than a Blink

When a secretary bird strikes, its foot is in contact with its target for a mere 15 milliseconds. That is roughly one-tenth of the time it takes you to blink — an eye blink lasts around 150 milliseconds. The strike is over almost before it begins, giving the snake no window to react or retaliate.

## Five Times Its Own Body Weight

Speed alone would not be enough to kill a snake. The secretary bird also hits hard. Researchers measured the force of its stomp at about five times the bird's own body weight. That combination — a blow delivered with crushing force in a fraction of a heartbeat — is enough to stun and kill dangerous prey outright.

## Why Precision Is a Matter of Life and Death

This bird hunts cobras and other venomous snakes, and against such prey a single mistake could be fatal. A strike that lands a little too slow or a little off-target could leave the bird exposed to a venomous bite. That is why evolution has tuned the secretary bird for accuracy as much as power: its aim has to be perfect, every single time.

## Studied in the Lab

Much of what we know about this strike comes from careful measurement. Scientists working with a captive secretary bird had it stomp on a rubber snake pulled across a hidden force plate, capturing both the timing and the power of each blow. The results, published in a peer-reviewed study, confirmed what makes this bird such an efficient killer: a fast, forceful, and remarkably accurate kick.
