# The Hippo: A Vegetarian With The Deadliest Jaws in Africa

The hippopotamus spends its days grazing on grass. It is a strict herbivore, eating nothing but plants. And yet, hidden behind that placid, barrel-shaped face is one of the most powerful bites in the entire animal kingdom — stronger than a lion's, and utterly out of proportion to a diet of leaves and grass.

## A Bite That Outmuscles a Lion

When a hippo's jaws slam shut, they generate a bite force of around 1,800 pounds per square inch. To put that in context, a lion — a specialized meat-eating predator — bites at somewhere between 650 and 1,000 pounds per square inch. The plant-eating hippo beats it, and not by a small margin. It is one of the hardest-biting land animals on Earth.

## Nearly Twelve Times Your Own Bite

The human jaw is capable of roughly 150 pounds per square inch. That means a hippo bites with nearly twelve times the force you can muster. It is the kind of power that turns a lazy-looking riverbank grazer into an animal few other creatures dare to challenge.

## Teeth Like Blades

The hippo's mouth is armed as well as it is strong. Its lower canine teeth are tusk-like and never stop growing, reaching lengths of up to 20 inches — longer than a human forearm. These are not chewing teeth. They are weapons, honed against one another into sharp edges used for fighting and defense.

## A Four-Foot Gape

A hippo can open its mouth to an astonishing degree — a gape of around 150 degrees, spreading its jaws roughly four feet wide in a single motion. That enormous open-mouthed "yawn" is not a sign of sleepiness. It is a threat display, a warning of exactly how much crushing power is on offer.

## Why It Earns Its Reputation

All of this power belongs to an animal that is famously territorial and short-tempered in the water. Those same jaws are strong enough to bite clean through a wooden canoe, which is a large part of why the hippo is considered one of the most dangerous animals in Africa. A gentle plant-eater on the outside, with a bite stronger than a lion's on the inside.
