# The Goliath Tigerfish: Africa's River Monster

When people picture a fish with teeth like a great white shark, they think of the open ocean. But one of the most fearsome sets of jaws on Earth belongs to a fish that lurks in murky African rivers — the goliath tigerfish.

## Teeth Like a Great White

The goliath tigerfish's most striking weapon is its mouth. Its jaws hold around **32 dagger-like teeth**, and each one can grow up to roughly an inch long — comparable in size to the teeth of a great white shark.

These teeth are not random. They fit into precise grooves along the jaws and interlock when the mouth closes, forming a trap that prey has almost no chance of escaping.

## A Giant of Freshwater

This is no small river fish. The goliath tigerfish grows to an average length of about **five feet (1.5 meters)** and can weigh **over a hundred pounds (50 kilograms)**. The largest recorded specimens have pushed past 150 pounds.

That combination of size and weaponry makes it the largest member of the tigerfish family and one of the most powerful freshwater predators alive. Its muscular body lets it strike with sudden, explosive speed in the fast-moving waters of the Congo.

## Monster of the Congo

The goliath tigerfish is native to the **Congo River Basin** and Lake Tanganyika in Africa. In these waters it sits at the very top of the food chain, hunting other fish with a bite powerful enough to slice through them.

It has earned a fearsome reputation among people, too. A number of incidents have been reported in the Congo of goliath tigerfish attacking humans, leaving substantial wounds with their strong bite and enormous teeth. A 2008 National Geographic documentary even dubbed it the "Monster Fish of the Congo."

In the rivers of central Africa, this shark-toothed giant fears almost nothing — a reminder that some of the planet's most terrifying predators are hiding not in the deep sea, but in freshwater we rarely think twice about.
